## Search Strategy (CEBMa-aligned)

### Databases to prioritize
- Business Source Complete / Business Source Premier (EBSCO)
- PsycINFO / Psychology & Behavioral Sciences
- Scopus (broad multidisciplinary coverage)
- Web of Science
- Google Scholar (use for forward/backward citation chaining)
- CEBMa textbook and course materials (use as method guidance)
- JSTOR/SSRN for working papers and older literature

### Search fields and syntax (TI() / AB() guidance)
- Use TI() to target title searches and AB() for abstract searches where supported. Example patterns:
	- TI("hybrid work" OR "remote work") AND AB("employee engagement" OR "productivity")
	- TI(hybrid OR remote) AND AB("work from home" AND (productiv* OR engagement))
- Use phrase quotes for exact phrases and truncation (*) for word stems (e.g., productiv*).

### Boolean best-practices
- Start broad with OR between synonyms, then narrow using AND. Avoid overly long single queries in one engine; break into focused queries.
- Use exact phrase searches for policy or role names ("work from home") and field-specific qualifiers (TI(), AB()).

### Redundancy testing and hit analysis
- Run the same query across multiple databases and record hit counts. If a focused query returns 0–2 hits, broaden terms or search fields.
- Document redundancy: which databases returned unique records vs duplicates. Keep a short table of hits by database and query.

### Example search strings (adapt per database syntax)
- Business Source Complete example:
	- TI("hybrid work" OR "remote work") AND AB("employee engagement" OR productiv*)
- Scopus example:
	- TITLE("hybrid work" OR "remote work") AND (ABS("employee engagement" OR productiv*))
- Google Scholar approach:
	- "hybrid work" "employee engagement" (use citation chaining and limit to recent years)

### Search filters to apply (document choices)
- Date Range: e.g., 2015–2025 (justify if narrower)
- Language: English (note limitations)
- Study Types: prioritize systematic reviews, RCTs, quasi-experimental, observational for context
- Geographic/Industry scope: note any restrictions and why

## Selection Criteria (CEBMa guidance)

### Inclusion Criteria
- Empirical studies reporting measures of employee engagement or performance in remote/hybrid contexts
- Systematic reviews or meta-analyses on remote work, hybrid work, or teleworking
- Studies with clear methods and measurable outcomes (quantitative or mixed-methods)

### Exclusion Criteria
- Opinion pieces, blog posts, or unreviewed white papers unless used for background context
- Studies focused solely on gig/contractor platforms if not analogous to the target population

## Screening and Documentation (PRISMA-style)

- Keep a reproducible log of each search: date, database, exact query, and hit count.
- Use a PRISMA flow approach: total hits → deduplicated → screened by title/abstract → full-text assessed → included.
- Record reasons for exclusion at full-text stage (population mismatch, no relevant outcomes, poor methods).

## Quality Assessment (study-specific appraisal)

### Design hierarchy (use CEBMa emphasis on study design relevance)
1. Systematic reviews/meta-analyses
2. Randomized trials
3. Controlled quasi-experiments
4. Longitudinal cohort studies
5. Cross-sectional surveys (useful for prevalence and association)
6. Case studies and qualitative research (contextual insight)

### Appraisal focus areas
- Internal validity: selection bias, confounding, measurement reliability
- External validity: applicability to your organizational context
- Precision: sample size, confidence intervals
- Reporting quality and transparency

## Data extraction template (suggested fields)
- Citation
- Study aims and questions
- Design and sample
- Setting and population
- Interventions/exposures
- Outcomes and measures (engagement/performance definitions)
- Key results and effect sizes
- Quality appraisal notes
- Relevance to project

## Redundancy testing & sensitivity checks
- After initial inclusion, perform sensitivity checks (exclude low-quality studies, or test effect of different inclusion criteria) and report changes to conclusions.

## Documentation process and reproducibility
- Store search logs (queries + hit counts) and extracted data in a single folder (e.g., /data/search-logs/).
- Keep a citation manager (Zotero/EndNote/Mendeley) for all retrieved records and PDFs.

## Challenges and limitations
- Note database access restrictions and any paywalled content. Use institutional library access or request articles via interlibrary loan when needed.
